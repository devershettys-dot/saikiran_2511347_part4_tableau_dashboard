# Dashboard Story: Retail Performance 2024–2025

**Audience:** Executive Leadership Team  
**Purpose:** Strategic decision support  
**Data Period:** January 2024 – December 2025

---

## Executive Summary

Over the past two years, the business generated **₹217 million in total sales** and **₹33.3 million in profit** (15.3% overall margin). The business is growing, with 2025 outperforming 2024, and Q4 seasonality is a reliable pattern to plan around. However, beneath the positive headline numbers, there are clear structural risks — particularly in the Furniture category and the discount policy — that are silently eroding profitability. This dashboard is designed to give leadership both the big picture and the tools to drill into the details.

---

## What Is Performing Well

**Technology is the profit engine.** With ₹154M in sales and an 18.2% profit margin, Technology alone contributes 84% of total company profit. Sub-categories like Accessories, Copiers, Phones, and Machines all consistently deliver margins above 18%. This is the business's core strength.

**South region leads the business.** At ₹64.7M in sales and ₹10M in profit, the South is the strongest region across both revenue and absolute profit. North is a solid second. The eastern and western regions are healthy but represent meaningful upside.

**Home Office customers are quietly the most valuable.** Despite being perceived as a smaller segment, Home Office buyers generate the highest profit margin (15.5%) of all three customer segments. Their average order behavior and lower return rates make them an overlooked strategic asset.

**Same Day delivery correlates with the highest-profit orders.** Orders shipped Same Day average ₹9,102 in profit — higher than any other shipping mode — suggesting these customers place high-value, urgent orders.

---

## What Is Underperforming

**Furniture is a low-margin, high-return drag on the business.** At only 6.9% margin and a 7.7% return rate, Furniture is the weakest category across every financial dimension. Sub-categories Tables and Bookcases are the worst offenders, each below 6% margin. The combination of thin margins and high returns means the true economics of Furniture are worse than the revenue numbers suggest.

**Excessive discounting is destroying value.** Orders with discounts above 30% generate negative profit on average (–₹1,601 per order). The current discount policy allows discounts up to 35%, which is a structural problem. As discount levels rise from 0% to 35%, average profit per order falls by ₹14,800. This is not a marginal issue — it directly explains a portion of Furniture's poor performance, as that category likely receives heavier discounting.

**East and West regions are underperforming their potential.** With similar margin profiles to North and South, these regions simply have lower sales volume. This is a gap-to-opportunity: the business model clearly works in these geographies, but reach or investment is lower.

---

## What Risks Are Visible

1. **Furniture's return rate (7.7%)** represents a dual cost: lost revenue and reverse logistics expense. If this is a quality or expectation-setting problem, it needs urgent resolution before it affects brand reputation.
2. **Discounts above 30% are profit-negative.** Without a policy cap, this risk will scale as the sales team grows or seasonal promotions intensify.
3. **Campaign channel data has gaps (~2% null).** Without full attribution data, ROI on marketing spend cannot be fully measured.
4. **No visibility into customer retention.** The data shows customer IDs but without tracking repeat purchases across time, churn risk is invisible.

---

## What Opportunities Are Visible

1. **Scale Technology in East and West regions.** The category's margin is strong everywhere — bringing more Technology sales to lower-volume regions is the highest-ROI growth move.
2. **Invest in the Home Office segment.** Targeted product bundles, faster shipping options, and personalized outreach could increase volume from the most profitable segment.
3. **Optimize or exit low-margin Furniture sub-categories.** Reducing or repricing Tables and Bookcases, or replacing them with Chairs (8.25% margin) and Furnishings, would improve overall category health.
4. **Promote Same Day and First Class shipping** to high-value customers — these ship modes correlate with higher-profit orders and may also improve customer satisfaction and reduce returns.

---

## Recommended Business Actions

| Priority | Action | Expected Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| 🔴 Immediate | Cap all discounts at 25%; require approval above 20% | Stop profit-negative orders; protect ₹1,600+ per order |
| 🔴 Immediate | Investigate Furniture return root causes | Reduce 7.7% return rate; recover lost revenue |
| 🟡 Short-term | Launch Home Office-targeted campaign | Grow most profitable segment |
| 🟡 Short-term | Expand Technology product range in East & West | Increase high-margin sales in under-indexed regions |
| 🟢 Medium-term | Review Tables & Bookcases product range | Either reprice for profitability or rationalize SKUs |
| 🟢 Medium-term | Build customer retention tracking | Identify churn risk and loyalty opportunities |

---

## Limitations of the Dashboard

- **No cost breakdown:** Profit is derived from sales minus stated profit; actual COGS and operational overhead are not in the data.
- **No customer demographics:** Age, acquisition source, and tenure are not available, limiting segment depth.
- **Return reasons unknown:** We know return rates but cannot diagnose why returns happen.
- **2-year window:** Long-term trend analysis and multi-year seasonality require more historical data.
- **Campaign channel gaps:** ~2% of orders have no channel attribution, which slightly understates channel-specific ROI.

---

## Suggested Next Analysis

1. **Customer cohort analysis:** Track repeat purchase rate and lifetime value by segment and region.
2. **Product-level profitability deep-dive:** Identify the specific SKUs within Furniture that generate the most returns and losses.
3. **Discount approval audit:** Review which sales reps or regions are applying 30%+ discounts most frequently.
4. **Shipping cost integration:** Incorporate logistics costs per ship mode to calculate true per-order profitability.
5. **Campaign ROI analysis:** Connect campaign channel to customer acquisition cost and lifetime value to optimize marketing mix.
